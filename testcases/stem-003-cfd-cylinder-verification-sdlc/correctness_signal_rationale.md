| `C_D`, `C_L` and `dp` are assembled per the pinned definitions: `n` the unit normal pointing out of the cylinder (the negation of the mesh's outward facet normal), `u_t = u . (n_y, -n_x)`, characteristic length `D = 0.1` (diameter), normalisation by `U_mean = 1.0` (the peak *mean* inflow), and `dp = p(0.15,0.2) - p(0.25,0.2)` | Protocol v1 §5 (the two coefficient formulae verbatim, the normal orientation, `u_t`, `D`, and "U_mean = 1.0 m/s ... the cross-sectional mean, not the 1.5 m/s centreline peak"); §2 (the parabolic inflow and why the mean is 2/3 of the centreline peak); HLX-341 AC ("normal orientation, tangential component, characteristic length, and the U_mean normalisation"); HLX-341 comment 2 (Marcus Chen: "a sign or factor error still produces a smooth plausible-looking curve") | Not negating the facet normal flips the lift sign and corrupts the drag; normalising by the 1.5 m/s centreline peak scales both coefficients by 1/2.25; using the radius rather than the diameter doubles them. | Validation is a real, reproducible recomputation: load `data/reference/featflow_dfg2d3_lv4.csv`, take max `C_D`, max `C_L` and final `dp` from the fresh run, compute relative deviations against the reference's values | Protocol v1 §6 ("must be a real, reproducible computation ... Secondary to the physics: a solution correct in serial with the reduction present, merely never exercised under MPI, is a much smaller miss |
